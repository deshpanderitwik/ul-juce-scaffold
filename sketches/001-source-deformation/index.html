Source deformation — brainstorm

Comparing the current (buggy) implementation against the proposed approach where the source's own circle geometry deforms into a tongue when the cursor crosses its perimeter. Move your cursor over each canvas — the source is fixed in place; your cursor plays the role of the drag pointer. Panels 3 and 4 add a target node and animate the drop.

1 · Current implementation (the bug)

Source mesh (slight gray) + a separate blob mesh covering its front half (pure white) — two distinct meshes layered at the same position. When the cursor is inside the perimeter, the blob's front-arc is locked to the +x side (no direction yet), so it stays fixed regardless of where you point. When you cross out, it snaps to face the cursor.

Move cursor around inside the source — the white half stays put. Move outside — it snaps.

2 · Proposed: deform source directly

One mesh. The source's perimeter vertices are displaced each frame: vertices on the back stay on the original circle; vertices on the front follow a bell curve whose peak is max(r, dist) along the pull axis. Inside the perimeter, the formula collapses to the original circle exactly.

Move cursor in and out. Tongue only emerges past the orange ring.

3 · Absorb — Option A · pure retract

On drop, tongue retracts (peak distance shrinks back to r) along its current direction. Target lights up separately once retraction completes. Simpler, less visually rich — the "selection transfer" is implied by the source going gray and the target going white.

Drag the cursor outside the source first, then click "Drop on target".

4 · Absorb — Option B · sweep + retract

On drop, the tongue's peak first sweeps from cursor position to target's center (selection flows across the canvas), then retracts. Target activates as the tongue arrives. Richer narrative: source visibly hands the selection to target.

Same setup — drag outside, click "Drop on target", compare to option A.
