Chewing gum break-off

Breaking the drag into phases. A: source's circle deforms into a tongue as the cursor pulls (no break yet). B: at the break threshold, a new circle pops out at the cursor and the tongue retracts — a sticky strand stays between them. C: stable two-circle state, strand stretches with distance. Last panel combines all phases plus the drop animation onto a target.

1 · Phase A — pure stretch (no break)

Source's perimeter vertices deform into a tongue. The apex tracks the cursor up to the orange break threshold. Past that, the apex stays clamped — that's where phase B would kick in.

Move cursor in and out of the orange ring. Tongue caps at the threshold.

2 · Phase C — two circles + strand (steady state)

Post-break state. Source is back to a clean circle. A bud lives at the cursor. A thin strand connects them, thinning as the distance grows — the chewing-gum stretch.

Move cursor away from source. Strand thins as it stretches.

3 · Phase B — break animation

The transition itself. Tongue retracts from cursor back to source's perimeter, while the bud emerges at the cursor (scale 0→1) and the strand forms between them. ~280ms.

Click "Break" to play the transition. Click anywhere on the canvas to reset.

4 · Combined — full drag with drop

All phases triggered by the drag itself. Stretch → break (auto) → follow → drop on target. On drop, the bud merges into the target while the strand snaps free and source returns to inactive.

Drag past the orange threshold, then click "Drop on target". Click anywhere on the canvas to reset.
