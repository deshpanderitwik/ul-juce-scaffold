Resistance formulations

Four ways to encode "the source pushes back" into the stretch. In each panel, drag outward from the source and watch how the apex of the tongue lags behind your cursor. The gap between cursor and apex is what reads as resistance. Sliders tune each formulation in real time. Same break threshold (90 px) across all four — what differs is how the apex gets there.

cursor  ·  apex tip  ·  dashed orange line = strain (cursor − apex)  ·  live readout at bottom of each canvas

1 · Linear (reference)

No resistance. Apex follows cursor 1:1. Included as a baseline so you can feel the difference.

a(d) = clamp(d, r, T)

no parameters

2 · Sub-linear power

Apex extends with diminishing returns as you pull. Smaller exponent = stronger resistance early.

a(d) = r + (d − r)p

3 · Asymptotic saturation

Apex approaches a hard ceiling (a_max). Past that, pulling does nothing visually — the material is "maxed out."

a(d) = r + (a_max − r)·(1 − e−(d − r)/k)

4 · Spring-damper (dynamic)

Apex chases the cursor through spring physics. Resistance is felt as lag in time, not just in distance. Move fast → big lag; move slow → catches up.

v += k·(target − a)·dt − c·v·dt  ;  a += v·dt
